AESIF WEB

With Didier Bigo & Francesco Ragazzi

What is the impact of European cooperation in terms of police, internal security and borders on the everyday security of the persons living in the EU area? We helped Didier and Francesco answering these questions by studying how different websites are related to a selection of queries in Google.

Research design

1. Researchers set a specific list of queries matching their research questions
2. We build a script to query Google and harvest each list of resulting websites
3. We build a bipartite graph of queries and websites and spatialize it in Gephi
4. Researchers classify the most cited websites and we interpret the map

  • Date: 2014
  • Researchers: Didier Bigo & Francesco Ragazzi

SITPOL

With the library of Sciences Po

The library of Sciences Po provides a heavily categorized list of French political websites to researchers. Librarians need to prospect new resources until it is exhaustive enough, and represent it as a map to compare its properties to the French political field.

Research design

1. Librarians provide a list of URLs as a starting points for their corpus
2. We train them to use our crawler Hyphe and they harvest the contents
3. Librarians define the right boundaries for each resource (full site, single page...)
4. Librarians prospect new resources using citations as a discovery tool

  • Date: 2014
  • Researchers: Dinah Galligo & Elise Chapoy

AMOUR2PRE

With Nelly Quemener, Eric Dagiral & François de Singly

Exploring the different ways viewers interpret TV shows such as the French version of "Farmer looks for a wife" by referring to their own love life and trajectory.

Research design

1. Researchers provide a series of hashtags to follow
2. We collect all the maching tweets every day during 6 months
3. We explore the results (24,000 tweets) with researchers to extract relevant patterns
Project still in progress :-)

  • Date: 2014-2015
  • Researchers: Nelly Quemener, Eric Dagiral & François de Singly
